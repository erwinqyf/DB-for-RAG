Introduction
引言

The Pilot's Handbook of Aeronautical Knowledge provides basic knowledge for the student pilot learning to fly, as well as pilots seeking advanced pilot certification. For detailed information on a variety of specialized flight topics, see specific Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) handbooks and Advisory Circulars (ACs).
《飞行员航空知识手册》为正在学习飞行的学员飞行员以及寻求高级飞行认证的飞行员提供了基础知识。有关各种专业飞行主题的详细信息，请参阅美国联邦航空管理局（FAA）发布的相应手册和咨询通告（Advisory Circulars, ACs）。

This chapter offers a brief history of flight, introduces the history and role of the FAA in civil aviation, FAA regulations and standards, government references and publications, eligibility for pilot certificates, available routes to flight instruction, the role of the Certified Flight Instructor (CFI) and Designated Pilot Examiner (DPE) in flight training, Practical Test Standards (PTS), and new, industry-developed Airman Certification Standards (ACS) framework that will eventually replace the PTS.
本章简要介绍了飞行的发展历史，阐述了美国联邦航空管理局（FAA）在民用航空领域的作用及其相关法规与标准，以及政府发布的参考文件和出版物。同时，内容还涵盖了获取飞行员执照的资格要求、可选择的飞行培训课程、认证飞行教员（CFI）与指定飞行员考官（DPE）在飞行训练中的重要作用，实用考试标准（PTS），以及全新的、由行业主导制定的"飞行员认证标准"（ACS）框架——该框架最终将取代现有的PTS。

History of Flight
飞行历史

From prehistoric times, humans have watched the flight of birds, and longed to imitate them, but lacked the power to do so. Logic dictated that if the small muscles of birds can lift them into the air and sustain them, then the larger muscles of humans should be able to duplicate the feat. No one knew about the intricate mesh of muscles, sinew, heart, breathing system, and devices not unlike wing flaps, variable-camber and spoilers of the modern airplane that enabled a bird to fly. Still, thousands of years and countless lives were lost in attempts to fly like birds.
从史前时代起，人类就观察着鸟类的飞行，并渴望能够模仿它们，但却缺乏实现这一目标的能力。逻辑告诉我们：如果鸟类那些微小的肌肉能够将它们托举到空中并维持其飞行状态，那么人类更强壮的肌肉也应该能够完成同样的壮举。然而，没有人知道鸟类体内存在着如此复杂的肌肉网络、肌腱系统、心脏结构以及呼吸系统；此外，还有一些类似于现代飞机机翼襟翼、可变翼型装置等结构，这些因素共同使得鸟类得以飞翔。尽管如此，在尝试像鸟类一样飞行的过程中，人类还是付出了数千年的努力和无数生命的代价。

The identity of the first "bird-men" who fitted themselves with wings and leapt off of cliffs in an effort to fly are lost in time, but each failure gave those who wished to fly questions that needed to be answered. Where had the wing flappers gone wrong? Philosophers, scientists, and inventors offered solutions, but no one could add wings to the human body and soar like a bird. During the 1500s, Leonardo da Vinci filled pages of his notebooks with sketches of proposed flying machines, but most of his ideas were flawed because he clung to the idea of birdlike wings. [Figure 1-1] By 1655, mathematician, physicist, and inventor Robert Hooke concluded that the human body does not possess the strength to power artificial wings. He believed human flight would require some form of artificial propulsion.
那些最早尝试为自己装上翅膀、从悬崖上跃下试图飞翔的"鸟人"的身份，早已随着时间的流逝而消失在历史的长河中。然而，每一次失败的尝试都引发了那些渴望飞翔的人们的疑问：究竟是哪里出了问题呢？哲学家、科学家和发明家们提出了各种解决方案，但始终没有人能够真正将翅膀安装在人类的身体上，让人类像鸟类一样翱翔于天空之中。16世纪时，莱昂纳多·达·芬奇在他的笔记中画满了各种飞行机器的草图，但他的大部分想法都存在缺陷——因为他仍然固执地坚持使用类似鸟类的翅膀作为实现飞翔的手段。[图1-1]到1655年，数学家、物理学家兼发明家罗伯特·胡克得出了结论：人类的身体并不具备驱动人造翅膀所需的足够力量；他认为人类要想真正飞翔，就必须依靠某种形式的人工推进系统。

The quest for human flight led some practitioners in another direction. In 1783, the first manned hot air balloon, crafted by Joseph and Etienne Montgolfier, flew for 23 minutes. Ten days later, Professor Jacques Charles flew the first gas balloon. A madness for balloon flight captivated the public's imagination and for a time flying enthusiasts turned their expertise to the promise of lighter-than-air flight. But for all its majesty in the air, the balloon was little more than a billowing heap of cloth capable of no more than a one-way, downwind journey.
对人类实现飞行的探索促使一些研究者转向了另一个方向。1783年，由约瑟夫（Joseph）和艾蒂安·蒙戈尔菲埃（Etienne Montgolfier）制造的第一艘载人热气球飞行了23分钟。十天后，雅克·夏尔（Jacques Charles）教授驾驶了第一艘气体气球。这种对热气球飞行的狂热彻底吸引了公众的想象力；一时间，热衷于飞行的人们将他们的专业知识投入到实现"比空气更轻"的飞行目标上。然而，尽管热气球在空中展现出了令人惊叹的壮观景象，实际上它不过是一种蓬松的布料，只能顺着风向向下"旅行"（即不断向下坠落）。

Balloons solved the problem of lift, but that was only one of the problems of human flight. The ability to control speed and direction eluded balloonists. The solution to that problem lay in a child's toy familiar to the East for 2,000 years, but not introduced to the West until the 13th century—the kite. The kites used by the Chinese for aerial observation, to test winds for sailing, as a signaling device, and as a toy, held many of the answers to lifting a heavier-than-air device into the air.
气球解决了升力问题，但这只是人类飞行技术所面临的问题之一。控制气球的速度和方向对气球驾驶员来说仍然是个难题。这个问题的解决之道其实隐藏在一种儿童玩具中——这种玩具在东方已有2000年的使用历史，但直到13世纪才传入西方，那就是风筝。中国人使用风筝进行空中观测、判断航行所需的风向、传递信号，同时也把它当作玩具；而风筝本身就蕴含了许多关于如何让比空气重的物体升空的原理。

One of the men who believed the study of kites unlocked the secrets of winged flight was Sir George Cayley. Born in England 10 years before the Mongolfier balloon flight, Cayley spent his 84 years seeking to develop a heavier-than-air vehicle supported by kite-shaped wings. [Figure 1-2] The "Father of Aerial Navigation," Cayley discovered the basic principles on which the modern science of aeronautics is founded; built what is recognized as the first successful flying model; and tested the first full-size man-carrying airplane.
相信通过研究风筝能够揭开飞行秘密的人之一，是乔治·凯利爵士（Sir George Cayley）。他出生于英格兰，在蒙戈尔菲埃热气球首次升空的十年前。凯利花费了84年的时间致力于研发一种依靠风筝形状的翼片来支撑重于空气的飞行器。[图1-2]作为"航空导航之父"，凯利发现了现代航空科学所基于的基本原理；他制造出了被认为是世界上第一个成功的飞行模型，并测试了第一架能够载人的全尺寸飞机。

For the half-century after Cayley's death, countless scientists, flying enthusiasts, and inventors worked toward building a powered flying machine. Men, such as William Samuel Henson, who designed a huge monoplane that was propelled by a steam engine housed inside the fuselage, and Otto Lilienthal, who proved human flight in aircraft heavier than air was practical, worked toward the dream of powered flight. A dream turned into reality by Wilbur and Orville Wright at Kitty Hawk, North Carolina, on December 17, 1903.
在凯利去世后的半个世纪里，无数科学家、飞行爱好者和发明家都在致力于制造能够自主飞行的机器。例如威廉·塞缪尔·亨森（William Samuel Henson）设计了一架巨大的单翼飞机，该飞机由安装在机身内部的蒸汽引擎驱动；奥托·李林塔尔（Otto Lilienthal）则证明了人类驾驶比空气更重的飞行器进行飞行的可行性。这一梦想最终在1903年12月17日由威尔伯和奥维尔·莱特（Wilbur and Orville Wright）在北卡罗来纳州的基蒂霍克（Kitty Hawk）实现——他们成功让飞机首次实现了动力飞行。

The bicycle-building Wright brothers of Dayton, Ohio, had experimented for 4 years with kites, their own homemade wind tunnel, and different engines to power their biplane. One of their great achievements in flight was proving the value of the scientific, rather than a build-it-and-see approach. Their biplane, The Flyer, combined inspired design and engineering with superior craftsmanship.
来自俄亥俄州代顿市的自行车制造世家莱特兄弟，花了四年时间研究风筝、他们自己制作的风洞模型，以及各种可用于驱动双翼飞机的发动机。他们在飞行技术方面的一个重大成就在于：证明了科学方法的重要性——而非仅仅依靠"动手试做"来解决问题的方式。他们的双翼飞机"飞行者号"（The Flyer）将富有创意的设计理念与精湛的工程技术完美结合在一起。

History of the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA)
联邦航空管理局（FAA）的历史

During the early years of manned flight, aviation was a free for all because no government body was in place to establish policies or regulate and enforce safety standards. Individuals were free to conduct flights and operate aircraft with no government oversight. Most of the early flights were conducted for sport. Aviation was expensive and became the playground of the wealthy. Since these early airplanes were small, many people doubted their commercial value. One group of individuals believed otherwise and they became the genesis for modern airline travel.
在载人飞行的早期阶段，航空业对所有人来说都是"自由"的——因为当时还没有任何政府机构来制定政策、规范或执行安全标准。个人可以自行进行飞行活动，并操作飞机，而无需政府的监督。早期的飞行大多出于娱乐目的。由于航空成本较高，它最初只成为富人的专属领域。由于那些早期的飞机体积较小，许多人对其商业价值表示怀疑。然而，有一群人持不同观点，他们的努力最终促成了现代航空旅行的诞生。

P. E. Fansler, a Florida businessman living in St. Petersburg, approached Tom Benoist of the Benoist Aircraft Company in St. Louis, Missouri, about starting a flight route from St. Petersburg across the waterway to Tampa. Benoist suggested using his "Safety First" airboat and the two men signed an agreement for what would become the first scheduled airline in the United States. The first aircraft was delivered to St. Petersburg and made the first test flight on December 31, 1913.
P.E. Fansler，一位居住在圣彼得堡（佛罗里达州）的商人，联系了位于密苏里州圣路易斯的Benoist飞机公司的Tom Benoist，讨论从圣彼得堡出发开辟一条新的航空航线的可能性：从圣彼得堡出发，通过水路前往坦帕。贝努瓦（Benoist）提议使用他的"安全第一"（Safety First）型气垫船；两人随后签署了一份协议，由此成立了美国历史上第一家定期运营的航空公司。首架飞机于1913年12月31日被交付到圣彼得堡，并完成了首次试飞。

A public auction decided who would win the honor of becoming the first paying airline customer. The former mayor of St. Petersburg, A. C. Pheil, made the winning bid of $400.00, which secured his place in history as the first paying airline passenger.
通过公开拍卖，最终确定了谁将获得成为首家付费航空乘客的荣誉。圣彼得堡的前市长A.C. Pheil提出了最高报价（400.00美元），从而确保了自己在历史上作为首位付费航空乘客的地位。

On January 1, 1914, the first scheduled airline flight was conducted. The flight length was 21 miles and lasted 23 minutes due to a headwind. The return trip took 20 minutes. The line, which was subsidized by Florida businessmen, continued for 4 months and offered regular passage for $5.00 per person or $5.00 per 100 pounds of cargo. Shortly after the opening of the line, Benoist added a new airboat that afforded more protection from spray during takeoff and landing. The routes were also extended to Manatee, Bradenton, and Sarasota giving further credence to the idea of a profitable commercial airline.
1914年1月1日，首次按计划进行的航空航班正式启程。该航班的飞行距离为21英里，由于遇到逆风，全程耗时23分钟；返程则仅用了20分钟。这条航线得到了佛罗里达州商人的资助，共运营了4个月，提供定期运输服务，收费标准为：每人5.00美元或每100磅货物收取5.00美元的费用。航线开通后不久，Benoist公司又新增了一艘新型气垫船，这种气垫船在起飞和降落时能更好地抵御水花飞溅的干扰。同时，航班路线也被延伸至马纳蒂（Manatee）、布拉登顿（Bradenton）和萨拉索塔（Sarasota）等地，进一步证明了这条航线具有盈利潜力的商业价值。

The St. Petersburg-Tampa Airboat Line continued throughout the winter months with flights finally being suspended when the winter tourist industry began to dry up. The airline operated for only 4 months, but 1,205 passengers were carried without injury. This experiment proved commercial passenger airline travel was viable.
圣彼得堡-坦帕的空气船航线在整个冬季期间都持续运营，直到冬季旅游产业开始萎缩时，航班服务才最终暂停。尽管该航空公司仅运营了4个月时间，但仍运送了1,205名乘客，且没有发生任何安全事故。这一尝试证明了商业客运航空旅行的可行性。

The advent of World War I offered the airplane a chance to demonstrate its varied capabilities. It began the war as a reconnaissance platform, but by 1918, airplanes were being mass produced to serve as fighters, bombers, trainers, as well as reconnaissance platforms.
第一次世界大战的爆发为飞机提供了展示其多种用途的机会。战争初期，飞机主要被用作侦察工具；但到了1918年，飞机开始大规模生产，用于执行战斗任务、轰炸任务、训练任务，同时也继续承担侦察职能。

Transcontinental Air Mail Route
跨大陆航空邮件航线

Airmail routes continued to expand until the Transcontinental Mail Route was inaugurated. [Figure 1-5] This route spanned from San Francisco to New York for a total distance of 2,612 miles with 13 intermediate stops along the way. [Figure 1-6] On May 20, 1926, Congress passed the Air Commerce Act, which served as the cornerstone for aviation within the United States. This legislation was supported by leaders in the aviation industry who felt that the airplane could not reach its full potential without assistance from the Federal Government in improving safety.
航空邮件的航线持续扩展，直到横贯大陆的邮政航线正式开通。[图1-5]这条航线从旧金山延伸至纽约，总里程为2,612英里，中途设有13个停靠点。[图1-6]1926年5月20日，国会通过了《航空商业法》，该法案成为美国航空业发展的基石。这一立法得到了航空业领袖们的支持；他们认为，如果没有联邦政府在提高安全性方面的帮助，飞机就无法充分发挥其潜力。

The Air Commerce Act charged the Secretary of Commerce with fostering air commerce, issuing and enforcing air traffic rules, licensing pilots, certificating aircraft, establishing airways, and operating and maintaining aids to air navigation. The Department of Commerce created a new Aeronautics Branch whose primary mission was to provide oversight for the aviation industry. In addition, the Aeronautics Branch took over the construction and operation of the nation's system of lighted airways. The Postal Service, as part of the Transcontinental Air Mail Route system, had initiated this system. The Department of Commerce made significant advances in aviation communications, including the introduction of radio beacons as an effective means of navigation.
《航空商业法》赋予商务部长以下职责：促进航空商业的发展，制定并执行空中交通规则，颁发飞行员执照，对飞机进行认证，建立航空航线，以及运营和维护航空导航辅助设施。商务部设立了一个新的"航空事务处"，其主要任务是对航空业实施监管。此外，"航空事务处"还接管了全国照明航空航线的建设与维护工作。邮政服务系统作为跨大陆航空邮件运输网络的一部分，最初负责建立了这一航空航线网络。商务部在航空通信领域取得了重大进展，其中包括引入无线电信标作为有效的导航手段。

Built at intervals of approximately 10 miles apart, the standard beacon tower was 51 feet high, and was topped with a powerful rotating light. Below the rotating light, two course lights pointed forward and back along the airway. The course lights flashed a code to identify the beacon's number. The tower usually stood in the center of a concrete arrow 70 feet long. A generator shed, where required, stood at the "feather" end of the arrow. [Figure 1-7]
这些标准信标塔每隔约10英里建造一座，高度为51英尺（约15.5米），顶部装有强大的旋转灯。在旋转灯的下方，还有两盏定向灯，它们沿着航线向前和向后照射。这两盏定向灯会闪烁特定的信号代码，以标识该信标塔的编号。通常情况下，信标塔会矗立在一个长度为70英尺（约21.3米）的混凝土"箭头"的中央；在必要时，发电机房会设置在这个"混凝土箭头"的末端。[图1-7]

Federal Certification of Pilots and Mechanics
联邦对飞行员和机械师的认证

The Aeronautics Branch of the Department of Commerce began pilot certification with the first license issued on April 6, 1927. The recipient was the Chief of the Aeronautics Branch, William P. MacCracken, Jr. [Figure 1-8] (Orville Wright, who was no longer an active flier, had declined the honor.) MacCracken's license was the first issued to a pilot by a civilian agency of the Federal Government. Some 3 months later, the Aeronautics Branch issued the first Federal aircraft mechanic license.
美国商务部下属的航空部门于1927年4月6日开始实施飞行认证制度，并颁发了首张执照。该执照的获得者是航空部门的负责人威廉·P·麦克拉克肯（William P. MacCracken, Jr.）[见图1-8]；奥维尔·莱特（Orville Wright）虽然已不再从事飞行活动，但他拒绝了这一荣誉。麦克拉克肯获得的执照是美国联邦政府下属民用机构颁发的首张飞行员执照。大约三个月后，该航空部门又颁发了首张联邦航空机械师执照。

Equally important for safety was the establishment of a system of certification for aircraft. On March 29, 1927, the Aeronautics Branch issued the first airworthiness type certificate to the Buhl Airster CA-3, a three-place open biplane.
对于安全而言，同样重要的是建立一套飞机认证体系。1927年3月29日，航空部门向Buhl Airster CA-3（一款可容纳三人的开放式双翼飞机）颁发了首张适航证书。

In 1934, to recognize the tremendous strides made in aviation and to display the enhanced status within the department, the Aeronautics Branch was renamed the Bureau of Air Commerce. [Figure 1-9] Within this time frame, the Bureau of Air Commerce brought together a group of airlines and encouraged them to form the first three Air Traffic Control (ATC) facilities along the established air routes. Then in 1936, the Bureau of Air Commerce took over the responsibilities of operating the centers and continued to advance the ATC facilities. ATC has come a long way from the early controllers using maps, chalkboards, and performing mental math calculations in order to separate aircraft along flight routes.
1934年，为了表彰航空领域所取得的巨大进步，并彰显该部门在政府体系中的重要地位，航空局（Aeronautics Branch）被更名为航空商务局（Bureau of Air Commerce）[见图1-9]。在这一时期，航空商务局整合了一批航空公司，并鼓励他们在已建立的航空路线上建立首批三个空中交通管制（ATC）设施。随后，在1936年，航空商务局接管了这些设施的运营职责，并继续推进ATC系统的发展。与早期那些仅依靠地图、黑板以及进行心算来协调飞机飞行路线的管制人员相比，如今的ATC技术已经取得了巨大的进步。

The photograph was taken in 1933. During Vidal's tenure, the Aeronautics Branch was renamed the Bureau of Air Commerce on July 1, 1934. The new name more accurately reflected the status of the organization within the Department of Commerce.
这张照片拍摄于1933年。在维达尔的任职期间，航空部门于1934年7月1日更名为"航空商务局"（Bureau of Air Commerce）。这一新名称更准确地反映了该组织在商务部（Department of Commerce）中的地位。

The Civil Aeronautics Act of 1938
1938年《民用航空法》

In 1938, the Civil Aeronautics Act transferred the civil aviation responsibilities to a newly created, independent body, named the Civil Aeronautics Authority (CAA). This Act empowered the CAA to regulate airfares and establish new routes for the airlines to service.
1938年，《民用航空法》将民用航空领域的职责移交给了一个新成立的独立机构——民用航空管理局（Civil Aeronautics Authority，简称CAA）。该法律赋予CAA监管机票价格以及为航空公司开辟新的航线服务的权力。

President Franklin Roosevelt split the CAA into two agencies—the Civil Aeronautics Administration (CAA) and the Civil Aeronautics Board (CAB). Both agencies were still part of the Department of Commerce but the CAB functioned independently of the Secretary of Commerce. The role of the CAA was to facilitate ATC, certification of airmen and aircraft, rule enforcement, and the development of new airways. The CAB was charged with rule making to enhance safety, accident investigation, and the economic regulation of the airlines. Then in 1946, Congress gave the CAA the responsibility of administering the Federal Aid Airport Program. This program was designed to promote the establishment of civil airports throughout the country.
富兰克林·罗斯福总统将民用航空管理局（CAA）拆分为两个机构：民用航空管理局（Civil Aeronautics Administration，简称CAA）和民用航空委员会（Civil Aeronautics Board，简称CAB）。这两个机构仍隶属于商务部，但民用航空委员会的运作独立于商务部部长。民用航空管理局的职责包括协调空中交通管制（ATC）、飞行员及飞机的认证、法规的执行以及新航线的开发；而民用航空委员会则负责制定相关法规以提升飞行安全、开展事故调查，并对航空公司进行经济监管。1946年，国会授权民用航空管理局负责管理联邦援助计划机场建设项目。该计划旨在促进全国范围内民用机场的建设与发展。
